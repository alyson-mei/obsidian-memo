## bing image of the day
### Jackson's Point Lighthouse on Lake Simcoe, Ontario
![Bing](https://img.peapix.com/7d27bebefd80423f88951651bc809e14_1080.jpg)

Lake Simcoe has long shaped the rhythm of life in southern Ontario. It supplied freshwater fish, supported trade and even served as a transport route in the canal-building days of the 1800s. The lake spans roughly 722 square kilometres and is the fourth-largest lake wholly within the province, after Lake Nipigon, Lac Seul and Lake Nipissing. It feeds into Lake Couchiching and, eventually, the Trent–Severn Waterway, which connects Georgian Bay to Lake Ontario. Originally named 'Ouentironk' by the Huron-Wendat people, the lake was later renamed by British surveyor John Graves Simcoe in honour of his father. Its islands—Georgina, Thorah and Strawberry—aren't just scenic spots; they hold community and cultural significance.

Georgina Island, for instance, is home to the Chippewas of Georgina Island First Nation and remains an active, inhabited territory with deep ancestral ties. South of the lake is Jackson's Point Lighthouse, seen in today's image. Constructed to replace the old day marker, it continues to serve as a navigation point and connects to the area's past as a popular steamship and resort stop.

## natural wonder of the day
### Chocolate Hills, Bohol, Philippines
![Wonder](https://di262mgurvkjm.cloudfront.net/b696b84e-ae59-4555-82da-20f6612b3c3a/M15155_uxga.jpg)

Imagine a landscape where over a thousand perfectly cone-shaped hills rise from the earth, transforming from vibrant green to a rich, cocoa-brown as the seasons change. This remarkable sight is the Chocolate Hills, one of the Philippines' most famous natural landmarks, nestled in the heart of Bohol Island.

Comprising approximately 1,260 to 1,776 individual mounds, these geological wonders are spread across a vast area, creating an almost surreal panorama. While their exact formation remains a subject of scientific intrigue, they are recognized as unique natural formations that captivate all who witness them. What truly sets them apart is their uniform, symmetrical shape and the dramatic color shift: during the dry season, the grass-covered hills turn a distinct chocolate hue, giving them their delightful name, while the wet season cloaks them in lush emerald.

Visiting the Chocolate Hills offers a breathtaking sensory experience. From various viewpoints, you can gaze upon the seemingly endless expanse of these 'chocolate kisses,' feeling the immensity of nature's artistry. The sheer number and consistent shape of the hills create a mesmerizing pattern that stretches to the horizon, leaving visitors in awe of this unparalleled natural spectacle. It's a vista that evokes wonder, making it a must-see destination for any traveler.
